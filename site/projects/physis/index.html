Physis

A simulator-first framework for practical physical AI.

Physis is Duckermind's engineering lane for physical AI. It does not pretend to be a finished robotics stack already. Its job is to define the right technical boundaries early: simulation, perception, control, datasets, and ROS integration.

Why Physis Exists

Physical AI needs a better spine than isolated robotics demos.

Many robotics efforts collapse because they mix embodiment ambition with unclear software boundaries. Physis is designed to lock down the engineering layers before scaling claims.

Simulation

Reproducibility first

Simulation gives the project a stable environment for policy, perception, and evaluation loops.

Control

Operational boundaries stay clean

Control is treated as its own module rather than getting buried inside demo code.

Data + ROS

Datasets and ROS connectivity are part of the architecture, not a late retrofit.

Framework Role

Physis is the practical infrastructure lane for the portfolio's embodied ambitions.

Where Titan explores the longer-horizon architecture of embodied intelligence, Physis holds the nearer engineering substrate. One asks the bigger research question. The other builds the simulator-first groundwork.
