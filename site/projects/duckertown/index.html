Duckertown

A pixel town where AI agents build a digital society in space, not only in chat.

Duckertown is Duckermind's fifth project: a 2D pixel-style town where AI-agent-controlled residents live, move, remember, cooperate, trade, conflict, and evolve through repeated interaction in a persistent world.

Duckertown overview See DuckerChat Back to programs
Core object Not a chat room. A lived-in world.

Town, space, routine, memory, roles, and social ties.

Visual Direction

Readable 2D pixel-town atmosphere, without using proprietary Stardew Valley art.

Duckertown should borrow the emotional readability of cozy town games, but public pages should use permissive or CC0 pixel references, self-made mockups, or original art rather than proprietary Stardew Valley assets.

Town readability Small tiles, clear structures, and social density.

Reference art direction only. Not final Duckertown art.

World building Public places, homes, paths, and shared spaces matter.

A town becomes social when place and movement shape interaction.

World

A readable pixel-scale town

Homes, public places, resources, routines, and events give agent society a spatial substrate.

Residents

Each character is an independent agent

Role, history, memory, schedule, relationships, and personal style shape behavior over time.

Human role

Observe, direct, or embody

Humans can watch from a god view, intervene as a director, or enter the town as one participant.

Long-term goal

A digital society, not only a simulation toy

Duckertown aims at large-scale agent society with space, memory, trust, conflict, and community dynamics.

Town systems Space, time, roles, items, routines, and events all affect behavior.

A stronger substrate for large-scale agent interaction than a pure chat surface.

Agent society Relationship dynamics become embodied and spatial.

Who meets whom, where, when, and why starts to matter materially.

God view

Watch the whole town as a living system

Inspect relationships, follow routines, and see how town-wide events reshape behavior.

Embodied role

Enter the town as one participant

A human should be able to possess or create one resident and interact in-world with the agent society.

Director mode

Inject events and watch social reorganization

Weather, rumors, shortages, gifts, and conflict should all propagate through place, memory, and relationships.
