How Duckermind Works

Build with systems discipline, not presentation theater.

Duckermind runs through four lanes: control, research, build, and audit. The same logic shapes the company, the repos, and the public site.

Control Research Build Audit
Reference Method

Learn from category leaders without copying any one of them.

Collected input 20+ official sites across AI companies, labs, and platforms.
Observed patterns Clear hierarchy, concise copy, visible substance.
Final choice Clarity first, futurism second.
Four Lanes

The execution system is visible by design.

Coherent products come from coherent operating systems.

Control

Choose the object and sequence the work.

Set scope, sequence, and priorities.

Research

Use real inputs, not trend osmosis.

Use credible sources and explicit uncertainty.

Build

Turn direction into working surfaces.

Ship real repos, real pages, real systems.

Audit

Challenge weak architecture before launch.

Catch weak claims, regressions, and release risk.

Three Critical Views

The redesign is defensible from brand, systems, and research angles.

The site had to do three jobs at once: feel premium, stay flexible, and still look credible.

Brand view The root site acts like a company homepage, not a portfolio blog.
Systems view The domain map stays stable as projects become real products.
Research view Method and source discipline remain visible on public pages.
Creative view The visual system is distinct from the earlier Agora-derived format.
Evidence Discipline

Source quality is part of the design, not just the research.

In AI, credibility is part of product design.

High-trust lane

Official company, platform, and lab sources

The design pass pulled from official sites across OpenAI, Anthropic, DeepMind, Google, Microsoft, NVIDIA, HAI, CSAIL, BAIR, and Mila.

Medium-trust lane

Public architecture, product behavior, and technical docs

The focus stayed on durable structure, not cosmetic trends.

Low-trust lane

Idea stimulation, never direct evidence

Low-trust sources may inspire, but they do not define truth.

Deployment Logic

Ship one coherent brand shell without overbuilding day one infrastructure.

Ship light now. Promote each host only when the product is ready.

Step 1

Serve the root site as a polished static atlas.

`duckermind.com` holds the company surface and project dossiers.

Step 2

Give priority child domains dedicated public surfaces.

`agora.duckermind.com` already has its own standalone surface.

Step 3

Redirect or proxy the remaining project hosts as needed.

The other hosts stay light until they need their own runtime.

Step 4

Promote one host at a time when the product justifies it.

This keeps the system clean and easy to evolve.

Next Step

Return to the portfolio map or open the lead product surface.

The best next jumps are the program architecture page and Agora.
