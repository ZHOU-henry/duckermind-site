first Duckermind draft or inheriting Agora's earlier page grammar.

The input set below was collected from official websites for frontier AI
companies, major technology platforms, and top research organizations. The goal
was not to copy any one site, but to identify durable patterns in how the best
teams frame ambition, structure information, and express technical authority.

## What The Best Sites Repeatedly Get Right

### 1. The homepage is a world-model, not a brochure

The strongest homepages make one large civilizational or systems-level claim
up front. They do not begin by listing features or burying the thesis in small
marketing copy.

Common pattern:

- one dominant hero statement
- one short paragraph clarifying the scope
- one deliberate visual field that suggests system depth
- immediate links to the major entry points

### 2. Information hierarchy is aggressive

Top-tier sites are not shy about hierarchy. They clearly separate:

- brand thesis
- flagship products or programs
- research or evidence surfaces
- calls to action
- company or institution narrative

This creates confidence. The visitor never has to guess what matters most.

### 3. The visual language expresses discipline, not noise

Even when the sites use gradients, motion, or large media, the best ones stay
controlled. They prefer:

- limited color families
- strong whitespace and sectional rhythm
- one or two dominant motion ideas
- carefully chosen typography with contrast between headline and system text

### 4. Evidence and substance are visible

The better AI and research sites do not hide the serious parts. They surface:

- research updates
- technical programs
- deployment stories
- product architecture
- safety or governance posture

This matters for Duckermind because the portfolio includes product, research,
signal intelligence, physical AI, and embodied systems.

### 5. Category leaders avoid generic SaaS structure

They rarely look like:

- hero
- three feature cards
- testimonials
- pricing

Instead, they feel like institutions with their own logic. The site structure
itself tells you how the organization thinks.

## Patterns Worth Borrowing

### From OpenAI, Anthropic, DeepMind, Google AI

- large thesis-led opening
- calm but high-contrast typography
- product or research lanes surfaced immediately
- editorial pacing rather than dashboard clutter

### From Microsoft AI, NVIDIA AI, AWS AI, Databricks AI

- visible platform framing
- clear segmentation by workload or surface
- confident use of proof, numbers, and deployment language
- modular cards that scale across many product lines

### From Mistral, Cohere, Hugging Face, Scale

- crisp product naming
- compact interfaces for dense technical information
- more credible links between models, tooling, and deployment
- fast scanning for sophisticated users

### From HAI, CSAIL, BAIR, Mila, Vector, AI2

- research legitimacy through structure
- visible programs instead of vague institutional claims
- public knowledge architecture that feels durable
- trust generated by editorial clarity, not gloss alone

### From Figure, 1X, Tesla AI

- future-facing industrial confidence
- selective use of motion and media to imply real-world systems
- stronger sense of physical consequence than software-only sites

## Patterns To Avoid

- generic dark glass cards everywhere
- a parent brand that is louder than the portfolio it represents
- pages that all share the same hero and card template
- treating every project as if it were already a finished SaaS product
- visual references that feel derivative of one existing Agora page

## Chosen Direction For This Redesign

The selected direction is:

`Editorial Systems Futurism`

The idea is to combine three modes:

- editorial clarity from the strongest research and institution sites
- product confidence from frontier AI company homepages
- industrial futurism from embodied AI and deep-tech brands

### Core Rules

- Use a bright, premium base instead of defaulting to dark mode everywhere.
- Keep dark surfaces for depth, hero staging, and high-focus panels.
- Make Duckermind feel like a systems company with multiple horizons, not a
  single-product landing page.
- Let each child page have its own accent and mission while staying inside one
  brand architecture.
- Show operating logic and portfolio structure as first-class public objects.

## Resulting Site Architecture

### Root homepage

- thesis
- portfolio map
- flagship programs
- operating model
- domain and deployment logic
- final call to explore the network

### Portfolio page

- relationship map between the five programs
- stage and output matrix
- domain architecture
- horizon-based explanation of why these projects coexist

### Approach page

- four-lane operating model
- evidence discipline
- shipping and deployment rules
- quality and audit framing

### Project dossier pages

Each project page gets:

- a differentiated hero
- a sharper thesis
- an explicit system breakdown
- first shipping outputs
- relationship to the broader Duckermind network

### Agora standalone page

- stronger commercial posture
- explicit demand to response to delivery flow
- sector proof surfaces
- clearer separation between marketplace rhetoric and execution reality
