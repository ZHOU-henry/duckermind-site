# Duckermind Topic Synthesis 2026-03-17

This file consolidates the current topic-level research frame behind the
Duckermind website pages for:

- Noesis
- Peras
- Autogenesis

It is not a random brainstorm file. It combines:

- current website topics
- existing high-trust source packs
- existing low-trust inspiration lanes
- additional official-source confirmation from frontier labs, open research
  infrastructure, and open-source project ecosystems

## Method

### Source discipline

Priority sources for this synthesis:

- official frontier lab pages
- official research institutions
- conference and review surfaces
- official APIs and open data infrastructures
- official GitHub repositories for relevant open-source projects

Low-trust and inspiration sources are used only for:

- early signal detection
- idea generation
- category discovery

They do not directly determine conclusions.

### Inference rule

When this file proposes research directions or open questions, those are
derived from the source base plus explicit reasoning. They are not direct
quotes from any one source.

## Noesis

### Frontier Research

#### 1. AI economics and infrastructure concentration

Current frontier:

- AI economics is moving beyond general productivity language toward:
  - labor exposure
  - macro distribution effects
  - infrastructure concentration
  - compute and energy bottlenecks
- Anthropic and OpenAI are both now treating economic consequences as a serious
  public-facing research / policy surface rather than side commentary.
- Stanford HAI AI Index gives a more structured macro view of model, compute,
  and adoption change.

Why it matters:

- This is the substrate layer beneath AGI-era social design.
- It directly supports the six-factor observatory line inside Noesis.

Key source anchors:

- Stanford HAI AI Index
  - <https://hai.stanford.edu/ai-index/2025-ai-index-report>
- Anthropic Economic Index
  - <https://www.anthropic.com/economic-index>
- OpenAI Economic Blueprint
  - <https://openai.com/global-affairs/openais-economic-blueprint/>
- OECD Future of Work
  - <https://www.oecd.org/future-of-work/>

#### 2. Institutional software and deliberation systems

Current frontier:

- Open coordination systems such as Pol.is, Decidim, and CONSUL show that
  large-scale public deliberation and governance interfaces are already real
  software systems.
- This makes the Habermas / communicative-rationality line materially more
  plausible because there are already technical substrates for discourse-based
  coordination.

Why it matters:

- It grounds Noesis in actual institutions and interfaces rather than abstract
  political theory alone.
- It supports the idea that AI-era coordination may require richer interfaces
  than prices and static bureaucracy.

Key source anchors:

- Pol.is
  - <https://github.com/compdemocracy/polis>
- Decidim
  - <https://github.com/decidim/decidim>
- CONSUL DEMOCRACY
  - <https://github.com/consuldemocracy/consuldemocracy>

#### 3. Substrate observability

Current frontier:

- Energy, research activity, and open-source engineering are now observable
  enough to justify a genuine observatory.
- Electricity Maps, GH Archive, OpenAlex, and public cloud infrastructure pages
  provide the backbone for a six-factor measurement system.

Why it matters:

- This turns Noesis from a pure theory project into a theory + observatory
  project.
- It provides a route to quantification, not just narrative.

Key source anchors:

- Electricity Maps
  - <https://app.electricitymaps.com>
- GH Archive
  - <https://www.gharchive.org/>
- OpenAlex
  - <https://openalex.org/>
- AWS / Azure / Google Cloud / Alibaba public infrastructure pages

#### 4. Machine agency and symbiosis

Current frontier:

- Frontier labs increasingly frame models as collaborators, agents, or
  semi-autonomous systems rather than only tools.
- This does not settle the question of subjectivity, but it increases the
  practical importance of role design, delegation, and institutional fit.

Why it matters:

- Noesis should continue to connect agency, legitimacy, and coordination.
- Human-machine symbiosis now has stronger grounding in actual product and lab
  movement.

Key source anchors:

- OpenAI Research
  - <https://openai.com/research>
- Anthropic Research
  - <https://www.anthropic.com/research/>
- Google DeepMind
  - <https://deepmind.google/>

### Open Questions

Source-grounded questions:

- Which social and institutional functions remain legitimacy-critical even if
  machine optimization improves dramatically?
- Which of the six substrate factors bind first in practice:
  compute, algorithms, data, storage, energy, or talent?
- Which economic domains are best coordinated by markets, which by planning,
  and which by communicative coordination?
- How much can AI-assisted public reasoning improve coordination without
  becoming elite-managed theatre?
- Which observatory signals should be treated as direct measurement versus
  inferred proxy?

### Research Agenda

#### Near-term

- refine the six-factor observatory methodology
- improve regional / factor / time-slice visualizations
- deepen the communicative-coordination economic model
- expand current-progress memos into a continuous frontier-tracking layer

#### Strategic inference

Noesis should become a hybrid system:

- conceptual AGI-era social research
- institutional-design program
- observability and dashboard surface

That is a stronger strategic position than staying only at the philosophy layer.

## Peras

### Frontier Research

#### 1. Reasoning and post-training systems

Current frontier:

- Reasoning quality and post-training remain decisive for system performance.
- The strongest model gains still move through evaluation, prompting/program
  structure, alignment, and post-training loops rather than raw pretraining
  alone.

Why it matters:

- This remains a top-ranked direction because it changes product capability
  quickly.

Key source anchors:

- OpenAI Research
  - <https://openai.com/research>
- Anthropic Research
  - <https://www.anthropic.com/research/>
- Google DeepMind
  - <https://deepmind.google/>

#### 2. Agentic coding economy

Current frontier:

- OpenHands and SWE-agent show that code tasks are now one of the clearest
  environments for model-driven workflow automation.
- This category has strong visibility across:
  - research
  - benchmarks
  - open source
  - product use

Why it matters:

- It is one of the strongest cases for a high-value, high-signal AI direction.

Key source anchors:

- OpenHands
  - <https://github.com/OpenHands/OpenHands>
- SWE-agent
  - <https://github.com/SWE-agent/SWE-agent>

#### 3. Open build ecosystems

Current frontier:

- GitHub and Hugging Face are major early indicators of ecosystem momentum.
- Open engineering activity is often a better signal than launch copy.

Why it matters:

- Peras should rank where implementation ecosystems are becoming real, not
  merely where narratives spike.

Key source anchors:

- GH Archive
  - <https://www.gharchive.org/>
- GitHub Search APIs
  - <https://docs.github.com/en/rest/search/search>
- Hugging Face Hub
  - <https://huggingface.co/>

#### 4. Infrastructure and macro

Current frontier:

- Compute, energy, capex, and infrastructure rollout increasingly shape which
  AI directions can scale.
- This means a ranking system that ignores substrate conditions will distort
  the real opportunity map.

Why it matters:

- Peras should become substrate-aware rather than staying purely model-centric.

Key source anchors:

- Stanford HAI AI Index
- SEC EDGAR APIs
- Anthropic Economic Index
- GDELT

### Open Questions

- What predicts durable value instead of transient attention?
- How should Peras weight research, product, open-source, and macro signals?
- How should uncertainty stay visible in rankings and thesis cards?
- Which adjacent domains deserve higher weight now:
  industrial AI, compute/energy infrastructure, governance stack, developer
  tooling, or AI economics intelligence?

### Research Agenda

#### Near-term

- refine the ranking formula and category-specific weights
- expand the source pack and keep provenance explicit
- publish more thesis cards and ranked directions
- backtest ranking logic against prior AI cycles

#### Strategic inference

Peras should not compete as a feed. It should compete as a directional judgment
system with explicit source and uncertainty design.

## Autogenesis

### Frontier Research

#### 1. LM optimization systems

Current frontier:

- DSPy and related systems show that LM pipelines can be optimized
  programmatically rather than treated as static prompts.

Why it matters:

- This is one of the clearest practical cases of AI improving AI-adjacent
  systems.

Key source anchors:

- DSPy
  - <https://github.com/stanfordnlp/dspy>

#### 2. Agentic coding as recursive testbed

Current frontier:

- OpenHands and SWE-agent provide environments where AI systems can iteratively
  improve code artifacts, workflows, and task completion loops.

Why it matters:

- Autogenesis needs concrete environments, not only abstract recursive
  self-improvement narratives.

Key source anchors:

- OpenHands
  - <https://github.com/OpenHands/OpenHands>
- SWE-agent
  - <https://github.com/SWE-agent/SWE-agent>

#### 3. RLHF and post-training infrastructure

Current frontier:

- OpenRLHF, verl, RLlib, and CleanRL show that scalable training,
  post-training, and evaluator loops are mature enough to study in detail.

Why it matters:

- These systems are the operational substrate for many recursive improvement
  experiments.

Key source anchors:

- OpenRLHF
  - <https://github.com/OpenRLHF/OpenRLHF>
- verl
  - <https://github.com/volcengine/verl>
- Ray / RLlib
  - <https://github.com/ray-project/ray>
- CleanRL
  - <https://github.com/vwxyzjn/cleanrl>

#### 4. Evolutionary and search systems

Current frontier:

- DeepMind now provides some of the strongest concrete evidence for AI
  self-improvement-like systems:
  - AlphaEvolve
  - AlphaDev
  - FunSearch
  - AlphaGeometry
  - AI co-scientist / Genesis support

Why it matters:

- This is stronger and more grounded than vague recursive self-improvement
  rhetoric.
- It shows multiple routes:
  - evolutionary search
  - RL-based algorithm search
  - evaluator-guided program search
  - scientific hypothesis and discovery assistance

Key source anchors:

- AlphaEvolve
  - <https://deepmind.google/blog/alphaevolve-a-gemini-powered-coding-agent-for-designing-advanced-algorithms/>
- AlphaDev
  - <https://deepmind.google/blog/alphadev-discovers-faster-sorting-algorithms/>
- FunSearch
  - <https://deepmind.google/blog/funsearch-making-new-discoveries-in-mathematical-sciences-using-large-language-models/>
- AlphaGeometry
  - <https://deepmind.google/blog/alphageometry-an-olympiad-level-ai-system-for-geometry/>
- Google DeepMind / Genesis support
  - <https://deepmind.google/blog/google-deepmind-supports-us-department-of-energy-on-genesis/>

### Open Questions

- Which recursive loops are genuinely improvable rather than hype-amplified?
- How should improvement candidates be promoted or rejected?
- How should evaluator systems themselves be trusted and calibrated?
- Where do classic meta-learning ideas still matter in modern large-model
  systems?
- Which loop classes deserve highest priority:
  - AI4AI toolchains
  - agentic coding
  - RLHF/post-training
  - evolutionary/search systems

### Research Agenda

#### Near-term

- map concrete loop classes
- define promotion and rollback control logic
- connect classic meta-learning to modern LM systems carefully
- expand the open frontier map continuously

#### Strategic inference

Autogenesis should avoid becoming a sci-fi repository. It should become a
careful research program for:

- measurable recursive gain
- loop classification
- evaluator quality
- safe promotion logic

## Inference Summary

Derived from the above source system, the strongest current strategic claim is:

Duckermind should not treat these three projects as separate islands.

They form a useful chain:

- `Noesis`:
  the social, institutional, and substrate frame
- `Peras`:
  the signal and ranking frame
- `Autogenesis`:
  the recursive capability and control frame

That chain is one of the strongest unique angles currently available to the
portfolio.
